About Me

My name is Allie Stanich, and I'm a neurodivergent PhD studentwho is passionate about disability and neurodiversity education. The goal of this website is to provide education and advice for disabled and/or neurodivergent college students.

I am currently studyng Composition and Rhetoric at Miami University, but I have a variety of research interests, including Disability Studies, Writing Center Studies, Folklore and Fairy Tale Studies, and Women and Gender Studies. In 2023, I graduated from George Mason University with a Master of Arts in English with a concentration in Folklore and a graduate certificate in Women and Gender Studies. I also hold undergraduate degrees in English and Music from the University of Mary Washington.

I am extremely passionate about learning more about the stories and arts that bring people from all walks of life together and raising the voices of those who have been previously silenced. I also have a love for writing, whether it be creative or academic.

I have five years of writing consultant experience three of which have been at the University of Mary Washington as an undergraduate, and two of which were at George Mason University as a master's student. While I worked at the GMU Writing Center, I created a resource for supporting disabled and/or neurodivergent writers and raised discussion about disability in the writing center. I am passionate about this work because it gives me the opportunity to help people who may not feel confident about their writing, and I strive to provide them with encouragement and support with my consulting, bringing in my own personal experience as a consultant and as someone who has invisible disabilities. Based on this wide array of interests, I have developed a variety of skills, including tutoring and writing blog posts for a company, a nonprofit organization, and a museum.

On another note, I am also passionate about the arts. I have experience as a performer (choir, theatre, musical theatre, and tap dance) and working with others through mentoring at music therapy and drama therapy camps. I love working with people and using my talents to help others so that they can feel like they are being heard and respected as individuals.
